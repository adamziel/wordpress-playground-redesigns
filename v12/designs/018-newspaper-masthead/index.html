The Coffee Shop

Vol. III · No. 22 · 24 May 2026 · A weekly journal of espresso, brew, and tea-room manners

Single-origin espresso, drawn slow on a spring lever.

The Bezzera Strega ran a 28-second pull on a Yirgacheffe washed lot. The body came up bright, with lemon-pith on the front and dark chocolate at the back — a clear, clean shot.

We pulled the lever twice for pre-infusion, then let the spring carry it. By the third pull, the puck was sweet and the crema went rust-coloured at the edges. We poured a 1:2.6 ratio into a Gaggia cup and tasted into the saucer.
