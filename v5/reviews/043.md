# Review of v5 #043 — Finder rail × Pull-tab

**Parent direction:** "Finder rail × Pull-tab. Finder-style left rail of saved playgrounds + a single iPad-style pull-tab on the right edge for the active site's Site Manager."
**Affordance count at idle:** 7 (counted in banner): refresh + URL + save-pill + fullscreen + kebab (5) + left rail + right pull-tab (2). Rail items + pane modes are content, not chrome. Well under cap.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 10 — 9 states (A working, B Settings + Apply&Reset confirm, C Files, D Blueprint + overflow, E Launcher, F Database, G Logs + saved toast, H Booting mobile, I Fullscreen mobile). 9 saved playgrounds in rail. Real blueprint JSON with 7 steps. 14 PHP log lines including Notice/Deprecated/Warning/Fatal/WASM-GD shim retries/WP_Error.
- Restraint: 9 — 7 affordances at idle, defended. Rail items are content (sites), not chrome.
- Iframe primacy: 9 — iframe 83% of width at idle (1192 of 1440). README rates 4/5 honestly: "-1 because at 70% pane detent the iframe shrinks to a 224 px sliver — necessary for pane-dominant work."
- One-door: 10 — Settings via pull-tab → Settings mode. New Playground via rail footer button. Save via name pill only. Site Manager via right pull-tab. Exports via kebab. Each feature has exactly one canonical home.
- One-secondary: 10 — exactly one swappable surface (right pane). Finder rail is *content*, not a panel — explicitly likened to Finder's sidebar being a catalog, not a chrome panel.
- Direction adherence: 10 — literal sum of two metaphors. Finder-style left rail of named items + iPad-style pull-tab with snap detents. Both structural, neither decorative.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — favicon-thumbs + name + runtime meta for each site, 224 px rail width, 24 px pull-tab + 30/50/70 snap detents, real Lucide icons, JetBrains Mono for code, opaque-by-default (zero decorative glass).
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 10 — extends #14 (Ventura sidebar) + #55 (iPad pull-tab). **Cites *seven* prior v5 reviews including three sibling-aware distinctions**:
  - #001 → pull-tab annotated with "Drag left · click · ⌘\\" hint; snap dots always-on at 30/50/70.
  - #002 → no stacked sections inside one drawer.
  - #006 → no glass, no Cmd+K, no spatial canvas, no Stage Manager dock.
  - #007 → destructive actions more discoverable — Apply&Reset opens typed-phrase confirm sheet.
  - **#017 sibling distinction**: "That design uses a single right-side pane that also hosts a 'Sites' mode (saved playgrounds inside the pane). Mine separates them: saved sites get their own persistent left rail (their *place*); the right pane hosts only Site-Manager tools."
  - **#018 sibling distinction**: "That design uses a 56-px monochrome rail of three abstract icons. Mine uses a 224-px rail of literal *items*, so the rail's role is content, not navigation-by-verb."
  - **#031 sibling distinction**: "That design's left gutter is a strip of verbs. Mine has zero verbs on the left edge; the rail is purely sites."
  Three sibling differentiations is the *highest density* in v5 — beating #021 (vs #004), #031 (vs #018), #037 (vs #011).

## What works (specific)
- **Three-way sibling differentiation** with explicit pixel/role contrasts (#017's right-pane-hosts-sites vs #043's left-rail-of-items; #018's 56-px-of-verbs vs #043's 224-px-of-items; #031's left-gutter-of-verbs vs #043's left-rail-of-content). Best sibling-awareness in v5.
- **Two edges, two purposes** — the design's core architectural claim. Left edge = sites (a *place*). Right edge = tools (a *drawer*). Neither edge holds verbs; verbs live in the slim top bar.
- **Save = the name pill, exclusively.** Click it = Save. Its color = its state. No separate Save button anywhere. Cleanest save-state-as-action in v5 (tied with #014, #023, #026, #027, #032, #035, #037).
- **The Finder rail is content, not chrome** — explicitly framed: "the Finder rail is not a secondary 'panel'; it's a persistent sites-list (content), much like Finder's sidebar is not a 'panel' — it's the catalog of your filesystem." This redefines the affordance count's basis honestly.
- **Hard-requirement checklist** in README is exhaustive: 9 saved sites, 7 blueprint steps, 14 PHP log lines, 9 states, all 6 sources, 18 of 43 blueprint cards, 10 category chips. Critic-grade evidence.
- **`⌘B` to hide the rail** for max iframe width — escape hatch for users who want pane-light work. Documented in the kebab.
- **Mobile rotates 90° preserving the gesture's perpendicularity** to the long axis: rail collapses to 28 px left peek strip + bottom-sheet expansion; pull-tab moves to bottom edge for one-handed reach. The *gesture* is preserved even though the surface moves.
- **Zero decorative glass** — only the confirm-sheet backdrop uses translucent blur, justified as "the running site is paused behind this irreversible decision." 5/5 on glass critique.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- **70% pane detent leaves only 224 px of iframe visible** (a 16% sliver). README rates 4/5 on iframe-primacy and admits the trade. For users in pane-dominant work (debugging logs, editing wp-config.php), 70% is reasonable, but the iframe being mostly hidden conflicts with the v5 §2 rule 1 spirit.
- **Mobile rail collapses to favicons only by default** (-1 honest). Site names appear only when the bottom sheet is expanded. Returning users on mobile see only icons until they tap.
- **The 224 px rail is permanent unless toggled with ⌘B.** A user who never discovers the toggle pays 16% of viewport width forever. Worth a more visible toggle, like a chevron on the rail itself.
- **The rail's 9 items at idle** means 9 visible saved playgrounds — but with thumbs, names, and meta lines each, 9 items takes ~600 px of vertical space. The rail will scroll past 9 sites; the spatial-list metaphor works best with ≤ 10 sites.
- **Database tab early-access notice** is correctly retained per brief but disrupts the otherwise-clean pane aesthetic. README acknowledges as "a genuine oddity."
- The **Finder-style rail uses actual favicons + names** — but what about the *Unsaved Playground* state? README notes a pinned "Unsaved Playground" card at the top of the rail in State E. Good — but a brand-new visitor with zero saved sites sees a rail with one item, which makes the metaphor look thin.

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Add a "Hide rail" chevron at the rail's top-right corner** (a 16 × 16 button) so users can collapse the rail without remembering ⌘B. The chevron is one extra idle affordance (count goes to 8 = at cap), but it gives users a visible escape from the 16% width tax. Worth the slot.
2. **For brand-new users with zero saved sites, replace the empty rail with a "Welcome — your saved sites will live here" placeholder card** with a "+ New Playground" CTA. This teaches the metaphor *immediately* on first visit — the rail isn't "empty," it's "ready for your work." Same idea as Finder's "No items" empty state.

## Lesson for the next designer
When your direction synthesizes two parent metaphors (Finder + iPad pull-tab), distinguish from *each* sibling design that took *each* parent separately — and quantify the difference (pixel widths, role of the rail content vs verbs). #043 does this for three siblings in a row and earns the reader's trust by mapping the design's *territory* against the rest of v5.
