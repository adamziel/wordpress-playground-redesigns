# Review of v5 #024 — Snap-to-iframe

**Parent direction:** "Snap-to-iframe. Secondary pane is always present at 30%; double-tap the iframe → it grows to 100% with chrome auto-hidden. Esc returns."
**Affordance count at idle:** 8 (enumerated in README): Refresh · URL bar (with embedded save chip) · Name pill · Kebab · Pane 5-tab strip · Pane mode toggle (Manage/Launch) · Bottom dock · "Double-click to snap" hint pill. At cap exactly.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 10 — all 5 SM tabs in pane; Manage/Launch mode toggle swaps the *entire* pane payload (launcher vs site-manager content); both Export PR + Download .zip in kebab; typed-phrase confirm + auto-snapshot for Apply&Reset; bottom dock with saved sites + "+" launcher; State 6 shows the surface twice side-by-side (Logs + Database) explicitly.
- Restraint: 9 — exactly 8; the save chip is counted *inside* the URL bar as one affordance (defensible). Dock avatars are one spatial group.
- Iframe primacy: 10 — iframe is 70% wide at idle, 100% at snap. Three ways to enter snap: double-click iframe, F key, Esc to return. The 100% snap dissolves everything except an Esc hint and save heartbeat.
- One-door: 8 — README rates 4/5 honestly: "the dock and the launcher's 'Your Playgrounds' both list saved sites — defensible as 'switcher vs catalog' but a critic could call it a duplicate door." Same critique as #018's Sites/Blueprints rail overlap and #015's /new vs /blueprints. Soft seam.
- One-secondary: 10 — single `.pane` component, 432 px wide on desktop, 35% tall on mobile. The full-launcher modal is the same component rendered larger — not a 6th panel.
- Direction adherence: 10 — "Snap-to-iframe" is the literal title of State 2, the literal label on the hint pill, and the kbd-shortcut copy ("Esc or F to return"). The 70/30 split *is* the IA.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — Inter font, real Lucide icons, real WP iframe, 5-tab pane with icons truncating to 3-letter abbreviations on mobile, glass on four named surfaces with exact blur values (16/8/20/8 px).
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 9 — extends #55 (iPad multitasking pull-tab) by *fixing* the ratio to 70/30 instead of detents. Closes #15 fullscreen gap with the snap gesture. README explicitly notes "I removed the dragged divider's labeled snap detents (25/50/75 from peer #017) because that's a different direction" — *acknowledging a sibling design's choice* and choosing differently. Good sibling-awareness.

## What works (specific)
- **Three ways to enter snap mode**: double-click iframe, F key, Esc to return. Multi-modal entry compensates for the gesture's first-touch discoverability cost.
- **"Double-click to snap" hint pill** is always visible at the iframe's bottom-right. Persistent teaching layer — same idea as #014's `⌘K` hint in the corner pill, but applied to the snap gesture.
- **Manage / Launch mode toggle** on the pane swaps the *entire payload* between site-manager content and launcher content. This is a clever compression: the pane is one surface, but it has two "personalities" — manage *this* playground vs launch *another* one.
- **The full-launcher modal is the same React component rendered larger** — README's claim. This is the strongest "one component" implementation in v5: pocket view (in pane) + modal view (expanded) share data and search.
- **State 6 deliberately shows Logs + Database side-by-side in two pane instances** to demonstrate "nothing about the surface changes — only the tab." This is a teaching state, not a multi-window state.
- **Glass usage with exact blur values** (16 px top bar, 8 px snap hint, 20 px dock, 8 px modal scrim) — each named and justified. Discipline at the v5 §2 rule 6 level. README rates -1 because "the dock could be argued to be safely opaque" — honest.
- **Mobile is the same gesture (double-tap)** — README rates 5/5 because "no 'mobile-only' affordances."
- **Apply&Reset escalates to typed-phrase + auto-snapshot .zip** — the most thorough destructive flow in v5 so far.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- **The 70/30 split is non-negotiable** (README). For a user who wants to peek at logs *briefly* without losing iframe canvas, there's no in-between detent. Snap (100%) or 70/30, nothing else. Compare to #017 (25/50/75 detents) or #023 (35/65, 50/50, 60/40, 75/25 smart-resize).
- **The dock + launcher Your-Playgrounds overlap** is the documented one-door seam. The dock is the "active-site switcher" and the launcher is the "catalog" — a defensible split but it costs one-door discipline.
- **Snap mode dissolves the pane entirely** (vs. collapsing to a 24 px tab). Once snapped, the user has *no visual reminder* that the pane exists — just an "Esc to return" pill. A user who forgets the pill's text might double-click again, thinking that's the toggle (it is, per README "or another double-click"). Good — but the pill is the only persistent hint.
- The **pane is fixed at 432 px on desktop**. 30% of 1440 = 432, fine. But on a 1920 px desktop, 30% = 576 px — does the pane scale? README doesn't say. Could be a fixed pixel value (432) regardless of viewport, which would feel small on large screens.
- **Save chip *inside* the URL bar** is novel and the README counts it as part of one affordance. A first-touch user might not see the chip *as the Save button* (they expect "Save" as a labeled button). Risk: discoverability of the save action. Hover state should make this obvious.
- The mode toggle (Manage/Launch) is one of the eight affordances but its semantics are subtle: it's not a tab, not a button, but a *payload-swap*. New users may not know clicking "Launch" turns the pane from "current-site settings" to "find another site." Worth a tooltip.

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Add a 50/50 detent for the pane on long-press of the snap hint pill.** Three states: 70/30 (default), 50/50 (long-press hint → pane grows), 100/0 (double-click iframe = snap). The long-press is non-obvious but matches the "gesture is the metaphor" thesis. Now users who want logs visible while editing have a middle ground without dragging.
2. **Unify the dock and the launcher's Your-Playgrounds list.** Remove the "Your Playgrounds" section from the launcher entirely — the dock *is* the catalog at idle. The launcher then becomes purely a "start something new" surface (six sources + 43 blueprints). The dock gains a hover-to-reveal metadata row (storage, last-opened) for catalog parity. One-door restored.

## Lesson for the next designer
A fixed-ratio direction (70/30) is *cleaner* than detents but commits you to the gesture being lightning-fast — make sure your snap entry has at least three modalities (click, double-click, key), one of which is always visible as a hint pill. Without the persistent hint, the gesture is invisible.
