# Review of v5 #018 — Quiet-rail-to-loud

**Parent direction:** "Quiet-rail-to-loud. A 56px icon rail on the left, always visible. Click an icon → pane expands to 320px next to it. Iframe shrinks. Click X → returns to rail."
**Affordance count at idle:** 8 (per README, counted in panel B): 3 rail icons (Sites, Site Manager, Blueprints) + logo + help + avatar + URL pill + save-state pill. Logo/avatar disclaimed as decorative. At cap exactly.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 10 — five Site Manager tabs each get a dedicated panel (D1 Settings, D2 Files, D3 Blueprint, D4 Database, D5 Logs). Three-frame quiet-to-loud transition (A1 quiet, A2 peek, A3 loud). Mobile state. Export + Download in More kebab (panel E). Sites pane has Yours/New/Import 3-way segmented. Blueprints pane is the 43-card gallery.
- Restraint: 10 — 8 exactly, defended. No surplus chrome.
- Iframe primacy: 9 — iframe gets ~96% of width at idle (1384 of 1440 = 96.1%). F3 demonstrates true fullscreen with Esc-to-exit pill. README rates 4/5 honestly because the rail is still 56 px wide at idle.
- One-door: 10 — settings unified to Site Manager → Settings; URL bar is the only navigation entry; exports live only in the More menu. README's claim holds under inspection.
- One-secondary: 10 — *literally* one 320 px pane that always opens in the same place. Segmented controls swap content. The pane geometry is *the* IA constant.
- Direction adherence: 10 — quiet=monochrome rail; loud=saturated pane with colored top edge and active-icon glow; the transition is a first-class state (panel A renders A1/A2/A3). Direction expressed *literally* with named visual semantics.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — Inter font, JetBrains Mono for typography num chips, solid (not glass!) dark rail and pane, colored top-edge accent per pane type, real WP iframe content. The 3-frame morph in panel A is a teaching device.
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 10 — extends #14 Ventura's clean sidebar + #55 iPad pull-tab + #64 DevTools top strip. **AND** the README explicitly cites *three previous v5 reviews* by number with specific lessons applied:
  - "Review 001 lesson — 'show the morph as a first-class state.' Panel A renders the transition in three explicit frames…"
  - "Review 002 lesson — 'don't ship three drawers stacked in one pane.' The Sites pane uses a proper sub-segmented control…"
  - "Review 006 lesson — 'don't import a clever idea from a different direction that undermines your restraint.' I resisted adding a spatial Figma-style dock, a Cmd+K palette, or a frosted Control Center widget…"
  This is the **best synthesis-with-prior-reviews of any v5 design**, by a wide margin. The "Lessons applied" section is the v5 brief's core promise made real.

## What works (specific)
- **The three-frame morph (A1 quiet, A2 peek, A3 loud)** explicitly applies the #001 review's "show the expansion gesture" feedback. The transition becomes a teachable state, not a hidden animation. This is the single best example of v5-as-refinement in the round so far.
- **Sites pane uses sub-segmented control** (Yours / New / Import) — applies the #002 review's "stop stacking three sections in one pane" feedback. The Sites pane is *not* three scrollable drawers; it's three discrete views with a top-level switcher.
- **Resistance to importing other directions' ideas** — the README explicitly names what it didn't do (spatial dock, Cmd+K palette, Control Center widget) and explains why. This is the #006 review's "lesson for the next designer" line operationalized.
- **All five Site Manager tabs rendered at full fidelity (D1–D5)**: Settings, Files, Blueprint, Database, Logs each get a dedicated panel. Addresses the v5 §3 requirement and the recurring critic note that Database is often a stub.
- **No glass anywhere** — both rail and pane are solid dark surfaces. The only translucent element is the fullscreen-exit pill. This is v5 §2 rule 6 ("glass earns its keep") taken to *zero glass* — restraint earns more credit than glass would.
- **Apply & Reset typed-phrase confirm**, no red banner. The destructive-flow brief requirement is met.
- **Mobile: rail rotated to bottom bar; pane becomes sheet at 62% of iframe area, iframe never below 38%.** Explicit minimum-iframe rule on mobile — addresses iframe primacy concerns that other entries hand-wave.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- **Three doors on the rail (Sites, Site Manager, Blueprints)** — but the Blueprints rail door overlaps with the Sites pane's "New" tab which also surfaces blueprints. Same soft seam as #015 between /new and /blueprints. README doesn't flag this but a critic reading the v1 brief sees the launcher containing blueprints in its "Start from a Blueprint" section.
- **The pane is fixed at 320 px and doesn't resize** (README: "restraint, not laziness"). Power users editing a long log line or a wide SQLite row will scroll horizontally inside the pane. Compare to #002 (drag detents) which gives users a way to expand temporarily.
- **The pane always opens between rail and iframe** — meaning the iframe shrinks every time. For users who keep the pane open all session (e.g., a dev with logs always visible), the iframe is permanently at ~75% width. Not a violation per se, just a permanent trade.
- **No keyboard shortcut layer described.** Other v5 entries (#009, #015, #012) lean on shortcuts. README doesn't mention `S` for Sites, `M` for Site Manager, `B` for Blueprints, `F` for fullscreen — would be a one-line addition that gives power users parity.
- **Help button + Avatar** on the rail are disclaimed as decorative but they still take rail real-estate. If they're not affordances, why are they on the rail? Move them to the URL strip or remove entirely; the rail's "quiet" thesis suffers when 2 of 6 visible items are inert.
- The README is well-written but the lessons-applied section is the longest part. If a future designer reads only the rubric, they miss the synthesis evidence. Worth lifting "Lessons applied" higher.

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Make the Blueprints rail door open the Sites pane with the Blueprints sub-tab pre-selected** — eliminating the third rail door and folding all three "find a site" surfaces into Sites pane. The rail becomes 2 doors (Sites + Site Manager) + icon decorations; the Sites pane's segmented control gains a fourth chip (Blueprints). One fewer rail door, one fewer cognitive choice. Compared to keeping three rail icons, this is a stronger one-door discipline.
2. **Promote keyboard shortcuts to the design's visual layer.** Add a `?` button at the bottom of the rail (replacing the avatar) that shows a one-time tooltip: `S = Sites, M = Manager, B = Blueprints, F = Fullscreen`. Solves shortcut discoverability without adding chrome — and gives the rail's two "decorative" items a real purpose.

## Lesson for the next designer
Cite prior v5 reviews *by number and lesson* in your README. This design did so explicitly and the result is the cleanest synthesis-with-feedback in the round — when you can show the critic "I read review #001 and shipped the fix," the burden of proof shifts from your direction to your refinement, which is exactly what v5 asks for.
