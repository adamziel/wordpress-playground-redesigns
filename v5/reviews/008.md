# Review of v5 #008 — Vertical reels feed

**Parent direction:** "Vertical reels feed. Swipe vertically through three full-screen 'pages': current site → blueprints feed → saved sites. Like TikTok navigation."
**Affordance count at idle:** 7 (listed at line 647): top bar (URL + name pill + save + refresh = 4) · right rail reel pips (1 cluster) · fullscreen toggle · "More" handle. Well under cap.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 8 — reel-page-1 shows iframe full-bleed, reel-page-2 shows a blueprint with full-bleed preview (only 1 blueprint per screen but the README says G flattens to a 43-card grid; sub-section "02·b" demonstrates the grid view), reel-page-3 holds saved sites; "More" sheet contains all 5 SM tabs. Apply&Reset typed confirm mentioned. The grid-view subsection should satisfy the "≥ 16 blueprint cards visible" requirement if rendered out — needs verification on the actual page.
- Restraint: 9 — only 7 idle affordances; the secondary surface ("More") is summoned, not visible. The pips on the right are decisive without being noisy.
- Iframe primacy: 10 — reel page 1 is *the iframe full-bleed*, the strongest expression of iframe-as-document in any v5 entry so far. Fullscreen toggle + ⇧F + Esc.
- One-door: 7 — README admits the soft seam: "start new sheet on reel 3 and discover blueprints on reel 2 are technically two ways to begin a session." The defense (custom source vs. curated) holds for me. But the "More" sheet also has a Settings/Files/Blueprint/DB/Logs tab strip — same inner-nesting concern as #005 — though the README defends this as a "one secondary surface" use.
- One-secondary: 9 — the More sheet is the only secondary surface that swaps content; the three reels are *primary surfaces*, not secondary.
- Direction adherence: 10 — three reels, vertical swipe, reel pips on the right edge, ⌘↑/↓ keyboard support, G-key flatten to grid. The metaphor is uncompromising and unmistakable.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — Inter font, Lucide icons, real WordPress iframe content (a "vueo." art gallery as page 1), blueprint cards with category dots, "Up next · 3 of 43" carousel hint at line 800, traffic-light mac chrome, gold "unsaved" badge next to name.
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 9 — extends #06 mobile-first (TikTok navigation is born mobile, scaled up), references #38 frosted glass for the sheet, and addresses v1 #15 fullscreen gap explicitly. The reel pips also offer a kind of #10 (Figma) spatial memory — three pages have fixed positions.

## What works (specific)
- Reel page 1 is the most committed iframe-as-document of any v5 design so far: the iframe *is* the page, not "centered with chrome around it." This is the direction's whole reason for existing.
- The "Up next · 3 of 43" preview chip (line 797–805) at the bottom of a blueprint card is genuinely TikTok-native — it tells the user there's more below without making them swipe to find out. Excellent feed-loop affordance.
- "G key flattens reel 2 to a grid" (line 808, "Browse all 43 as grid") is a thoughtful escape valve for users who want to *scan* rather than *swipe*. The same data, two visualizations. README states this is the brief's required "43-card with 10 filters" surface.
- Gold "unsaved" badge → green "saved" badge (line 657, 676 via CSS class swap) is high-contrast save-state messaging that survives a small-screen squeeze.
- The "More" tab is a *handle*, not a button — it visually invites a pull gesture, matching the reel's swipe ergonomics. Discoverability is decent because the handle is always visible.
- Annotation cards at line 715–720 explicitly call out (A) reel rail = spatial memory, (B) top bar ≤ 44 px, (C) save state, (D) fullscreen — designer doing the critic's work.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- **The brief says "Full 43-blueprint browser with search + 10 category filters (≥ 16 cards visible)."** Reel 2 shows one card at a time; the grid flatten (02·b) should be the surface where 16+ cards are visible. I cannot verify the grid renders 16+ cards without scrolling further in the file — the README claims it does, but the directive is "visible" not "scrollable." This is a brief-compliance risk.
- The reel rail (line 675–679) has only 3 pips for 3 reels. But the *blueprint* reel itself contains 43 sub-pages — there's no scrubber for *within* a reel. Swiping vertically through 43 blueprints could become a marathon; the design needs a sub-scrubber or category filter strip *inside* reel 2's chrome.
- "More" sheet (line 707) contains all 5 SM tabs — same tabs-inside-sheet nesting as #005. README defends as "one secondary surface," but functionally it's a tab-bar-inside-a-sheet, which is one level of hierarchy more than the direction's clean "swipe between pages" promise.
- A power desktop user on reel 1 can't see reel 3 — there's no peek/pivot. The reel pips show *position* but not *content preview*. Compare to TikTok: you peek the next reel as you swipe; here it's not shown until you commit.
- The "Up next" affordance only exists in reel 2 (line 797–805); reel 1 and reel 3 don't have peeks. Inconsistent application of the most-promising metaphor element.
- Save lives in the top bar (line 667), but the "Unsaved" badge (line 657) is also in the top bar as part of the name. The badge is *status*, the button is *action* — but a user might click the badge expecting it to be the save button (especially since it's a colored badge near the name, and the save button is further right). Sharper visual differentiation needed.

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Add reel-1 and reel-3 peeks.** Show a thin (12 px) strip of reel 2's blueprint card under reel 1's iframe, and a strip of reel 1's iframe above reel 3's saved-sites grid. This makes the spatial memory *visible*, not just inferable from the pips, and matches TikTok's actual peek behavior.
2. **Replace the More sheet's 5 inner tabs with a Cmd+K palette filtered to "this site's"** Settings / Files / Blueprint / DB / Logs commands. The More handle then opens a single-purpose palette rather than another tab-bar nested in a sheet. The reels are the navigation; commands are commands. This eliminates the tabs-inside-sheet nesting.

## Lesson for the next designer
If your direction is a feed-style swipe, *show the seam* between reels (a 12 px peek of the next page) — pips alone teach position but not content. The peek is what makes the metaphor feel native rather than borrowed.
