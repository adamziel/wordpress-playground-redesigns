# Review of v5 #006 — Pull-down for chrome

**Parent direction:** "Pull-down for chrome. Safari-style. The iframe is the entire screen by default; pull down from the top to expose URL, save, menu. Release to retract."
**Affordance count at idle:** 3 interactive (pull-handle, saved-state pill, fullscreen pill) + 1 spatial dock cluster (4 saved-site avatars) + a "Pull for chrome" chevron hint = **3 primary affordances**, plus a passive 4-item dock. Honest cap: 4-7 depending on whether the spatial dock is counted as one or four.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 9 — all six start sources, 43-blueprint count with 10 filters and 18 cards visible, all 5 Site Manager tabs, export/download surfaced in More, typed-phrase Apply&Reset, three+ states.
- Restraint: 9 — true to the directive; one-gesture IA, idle is the iframe.
- Iframe primacy: 10 — chrome always overlays, never reflows; explicit FS pill + F/Esc shortcut; the iframe is literally the only solid surface.
- One-door: 8 — settings, save, launcher, Site Manager each have a single canonical entry. Mild double-up: saved status appears as both the top-left pill *and* a dot inside the name pill (designer flags this honestly).
- One-secondary: 9 — Site Manager and launcher share the *same* sheet element with detents and tab-strip swap.
- Direction adherence: 10 — the entire IA is the pull-down. Three detents map exactly to peek/launcher/sheet.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — real Inter + JetBrains Mono, real Lucide icons, blueprint JSON with schema URL and real step names, realistic PHP logs with timestamps and DEBUG/INFO/WARN/ERROR levels, custom WordPress "Roast & Bean" coffee site rendered behind chrome.
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 10 — explicitly cites #06 mobile-first ("Mobile = wider mobile for desktop"), uses byte-for-byte same components across phone and desktop, closes the v1 #15 fullscreen gap with the FS pill and never-reflowing overlay.

## What works (specific)
- The pull-down handle + chevron hint at line 619/623 turns the discoverability problem into a learnable affordance — far better than hiding chrome behind invisible edges.
- Apply & Reset escalates correctly: typed-phrase confirm (line 1075–1085) where the required phrase is the *playground name* — muscle memory cannot dismiss it. Auto-downloaded pre-reset .zip snapshot is a thoughtful recoverable-but-irreversible detail.
- "Saved" pill changes to "Unsaved Playground" with amber styling during boot (line 864), making the save-state delta legible without taking a permanent slot in chrome.
- Booting state (state 4) keeps the peek detent auto-pinned during boot — a smart context-aware deviation from the pure idle behavior.
- The launcher's *left rail* genuinely swaps the *right pane* content (Start new sources / Blueprints / Your playgrounds) — this is one-secondary done right rather than three drawers.
- The "Affordance budget" countcard at the bottom (line 1263) makes the audit visible to the reviewer; designer counted themselves honestly.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- The "spatial saved-sites dock" (line 626) reads like a v1 winner mashup (Figma-canvas spatial memory) bolted onto a mobile-first design. It's purely decorative here — clicking a thumbnail isn't shown — and adds idle affordances that the direction (#5) says should *not* exist. Either commit to it as functional (with a real x/y restore demo) or remove it; right now it's spec-bloat.
- "Database" tab is listed in the sheet head but never demonstrated in its own state. State 5 shows Logs, state 6 shows Blueprint — the brief explicitly requires "each [tab] has visible content in your demo."
- "WP Admin" and "Homepage" jump buttons only appear in the Mobile-C Settings inset (line 1230); on the desktop sheet head (line 939–940) they're collapsed into icon buttons labeled only by `title=` — undiscoverable.
- The pull-handle is a 1px line on desktop. At 1440 px wide a hairline grabber will be missed entirely on first encounter; the chevron hint mitigates but the handle itself needs visual mass.
- The "Sites" toolbar chip and the spatial dock both open saved-site browsers — they overlap with the launcher's "Your playgrounds" rail. Three doors to the same room.

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Cut or commit on the spatial dock.** If you keep the Figma-canvas spatial layer, show it as the *primary* saved-sites surface (with stored x/y per playground, a "back to canvas" gesture, and a zoom interaction) and remove the "Sites" toolbar chip and the launcher's "Your playgrounds" rail item. If you don't want that complexity, delete the dock entirely — the design is stronger without the borrowed idea.
2. **Show every Site Manager tab.** Add a Database state (real SQLite table, "early access" notice) and explicitly show the Files tab editor with a PHP file open. The Blueprint tab already nails it; the others deserve the same fidelity. Even a single composite "all tabs, mini-previews" frame would close the gap.

## Lesson for the next designer
If your direction is "one gesture, one surface," resist the urge to import a clever idea from a different v1 winner — the spatial dock here costs idle affordances and creates a duplicate door, undermining the very restraint the direction is testing for.
