# Review of v5 #010 — Ambient Side Rails

**Parent direction:** "Ambient side rails. Two 24px gradient strips on the iframe's left/right that glow the WordPress theme colors. Hover reveals hidden chevrons → side panels."
**Affordance count at idle:** 8 (enumerated in self-critique): site name+dot · URL pill · refresh-in-pill · save-in-pill · fullscreen · menu · left rail · right rail. The "WP 6.7 · PHP 8.3" version chip is read-only text, not an affordance. At cap exactly.

**Scores (out of 10):**
- Feature completeness: 9 — 10 scenes covering working session, right rail open (Blueprint), left rail open (Launcher with sources/saved/featured), full blueprint gallery (43 cards · 10 categories), booting, Apply&Reset typed confirm, menu with Export PR + Download .zip, all 5 SM tabs (Settings/Files/Blueprint/DB/Logs) in scene 8, mobile two-tint ambient demonstration, fullscreen with Esc.
- Restraint: 9 — 8 idle affordances with only *two* "explicit doors" (the rails); everything else is in the URL pill or the menu.
- Iframe primacy: 8 — iframe is full bleed minus 40 px top bar and 2×24 px rails = ~96% of width. README rates this 4/5 honestly: "rails are still technically intrusive on the iframe's edges at idle." When right pane opens (scene 2), iframe compresses to `right:520px` of width — that's 1440-520-40 ≈ 880 px of iframe. Reasonable.
- One-door: 8 — Settings has one home (right pane Settings tab); launcher has one home (left rail). README admits WP-Admin/Homepage live in *two* places (URL pill + Site Manager header), but defends the URL pill as a free side-effect of address-bar typing — fair. Export to PR + Download .zip live only in the kebab.
- One-secondary: 8 — README admits the `.pane` component can dock left *or* right, and both can be visible simultaneously (rare). That's two instances of the same surface, which technically violates "one secondary surface." Defensible because they hold different content (Launcher vs Site Manager) and the design owns this trade-off.
- Direction adherence: 10 — ambient side rails are the namesake and load-bearing. Color sampling demonstrated with two iframes (Coffee Shop blue, Art Gallery warm orange) on mobile in scene 9. The five tickmarks on the right rail showing which SM tab is active (line 549–551) is a brilliant *passive status indicator* — section presence without expansion.
- Visual fidelity: 9 — Inter font, JetBrains Mono for version chip, real Lucide icons, iframe content carrying "Mornings Coffee Co." brand with realistic e-commerce nav (Menu/Beans/Subscriptions/Locations/Journal) and product cards (Ethiopia Yirgacheffe / Colombia Huila / Decaf Brazil), the rails *do* visually echo the iframe theme color via the gradient.
- Synthesis with v1 lesson: 9 — directly extends #38 (glass), but elevates it: rails are *not* glass — they're "color-sampled extensions of the WordPress site rather than glassy add-ons floating above it." That's the v5 §2 rule 6 fix to "glass without purpose." Also extends #55 (pull-tab, iPad multitasking) and addresses #15 (fullscreen toggle).

## What works (specific)
- **The right rail's 5 tickmarks** (line 549–551) showing which Site Manager tab is *active* without expanding anything is the design's most novel idea: it tells you *where you are* even when the rail is collapsed. Passive status = restraint earned.
- **Color sampling, demonstrated twice.** Scene 9 mobile shows Coffee Shop's blue ambient rails vs Art Gallery's warm orange. The direction is verified, not just claimed.
- The README is unusually honest: it ranks Iframe primacy at 4/5 because rails *are* intrusive, the side-by-side pane case at 4/5 because two can be visible, and admits the 28px blur could be 18-20. This level of self-critique is rare and signals a designer who knows their own design's weak points.
- The save-state chip lives inside the URL pill (line 507): `<span class="save saved">…Saved 2m ago</span>`. Status + time = useful, and there's no separate "Save" button anywhere — the chip *is* the door (clicking it presumably saves; the README says the chip "always does it"). Cleaner than #003's slider-plus-pill duplication.
- Scene 6 (Apply & Reset) is a typed-phrase confirm — the brief's stronger destructive-flow.
- Scene 9 (Mobile): same rails, same top bar, same pane component — and the dual-tint demo proves the metaphor holds at small widths.

## What's weak or missing (specific)
- The rails' discoverability problem is real for *first-time users*: a 24 px vertical strip with subtle gradient may not read as a clickable thing. README notes the "5" badge on the left rail and the 1px seam line — both help, but the right rail relies on tickmarks alone, and tickmarks read as decoration first. A small "›" chevron on hover (which the direction itself prescribes) would close this gap; the design's idle state should still hint at clickability.
- When the iframe content is mostly white (e.g., wp-admin), the rails read as thin gray borders rather than colored extensions. README admits this. Falls back to gray which is a degraded mode — but "5" badge and tickmarks still provide function.
- "WP-admin/Homepage in two places" (URL pill + SM header) is a real one-door violation. The defense ("URL pill is a free side-effect of address-bar typing") is honest but two visible button-like entry points still count.
- The pane component can render twice (left + right simultaneously) — README admits this and demonstrates only in "side-crop scenes." A strict reading of v5 §2 rule 4 ("one secondary surface, swaps content") would call this two surfaces.
- The five tickmarks on the right rail are *unlabeled dots*. README admits "first-time users probably won't read them as Settings/Files/Blueprint/DB/Logs." A long-press tooltip helps but the gap remains in the idle state.
- The version chip ("WP 6.7 · PHP 8.3") is presented as a non-affordance, but it's near the playground name and visually peer to the URL pill. New users will try to click it; nothing happens. Either make it a deep link to Settings or render it more clearly as static text (gray, smaller, lower contrast).

## How to push harder (1–2 pointed suggestions)
1. **Add a brief icon set to the right rail's tickmarks at idle, not just on hover.** Each tickmark becomes a 10 px Lucide icon (sliders / folder / braces / database / terminal) at low opacity, with the active one full-opacity. The rail's passive-status promise becomes actually-legible without expansion. Doesn't cost affordance budget — it's labeling existing affordances.
2. **Forbid both panes open simultaneously by IA design**: make the same `.pane` component an exclusive overlay. Opening the right rail's pane collapses the left rail's pane to its default closed state (and vice versa). README admits the dual-pane case is "rare, demonstrated only in side-crop scenes" — formalize that into the IA rule so v5's one-secondary-surface principle is met absolutely.

## Lesson for the next designer
If your direction uses *passive status indicators* (tickmarks, dots, ambient color), give each one at least a single-glyph icon at idle — otherwise the status reads as decoration, and your design's most clever idea becomes invisible to first-time users.
