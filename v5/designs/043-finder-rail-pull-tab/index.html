RAIL ARCHITECTURE

Why two edges, not three doors?

The left rail and the right pull-tab carry completely disjoint information:

  • Left rail = your saved playgrounds. Persistent. Spatial. Like Finder's Favorites. You navigate between sites here.
  • Right pull-tab = the active site's tools. Summoned. Content-swap. Five Site-Manager modes inside.
  • Top bar = the address strip. Where you are, save state, fullscreen, overflow.

The triple-entry-point sin (popover + sidebar tab + info-notice) is killed: there is exactly one settings door — pull-tab → Settings mode. The "Save site locally" info notice is replaced by the amber pip in the name pill (status) and a persistent Unsaved Playground card pinned to the top of the Finder rail (in State E).

Apply & Reset is destructive: it requires a typed confirm sheet (State B), not a small red banner.
