Why ⌘K, specifically

Three v1 sins this directly fixes:
  • Triple-entry-point. Settings lived as popover + sidebar tab + info-notice. Here: /settings is the single home. The save-state pill is a cue, not a duplicate of /save — clicking it focuses the palette to the save row.
  • The iframe disappears behind chrome. At idle, three small pills float on top of the iframe — nothing reflows. Fullscreen removes even those.
  • "What can I do here?" Press ⌘K. The palette literally lists every action with a keyboard hint next to each. New users learn keystrokes by reading the palette; power users skip the palette entirely with G A / ⌘ S / F.
Trade-off accepted: this design assumes a keyboard or one extra tap. The ⌘K hint is the price of admission. Mobile users tap the hint pill — it's always there, always thumb-reachable.
