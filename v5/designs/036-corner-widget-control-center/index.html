v5 · #036 · Corner-widget Control Center

The chrome stays at the corners. The iframe stays the document.

Four frosted square widgets stand sentinel in the corners of the viewport. Each widget owns exactly one Playground concern — Sites top-left, Settings top-right, Logs bottom-left, Site Manager bottom-right — and each carries a live micro-status on its 128px face so users read it like a dashboard. Click a widget and it expands toward the center as a glass quadrant panel, never covering more than its own corner’s half of the screen. The iframe is never reflowed.

The only other chrome at idle is a slim glass URL pill on top — refresh, address, saved-state name-pill, fullscreen toggle. That gives 8 affordances at idle, counted: four corners, four pill items, and the iframe itself is the document. Eight.

Notes for the critic · The four widgets are the entire site IA. Each widget is the one canonical door to its quadrant’s feature — Sites, Settings, Logs, Manager. The URL pill is the only other persistent chrome and carries the remaining four affordances (refresh, address field, save-state name pill, fullscreen). Total at idle: 8, enumerated in frame A’s count strip. Apply&Reset uses a typed-phrase confirm (frame D) — small red banner would have failed v5 §2 rule 8. The Manager owns Export (frame H), WP Admin, Homepage, and Files / Blueprint / Database tabs — eliminating the triple-entry-point. Glass is purposeful: it lets the WordPress iframe colors bleed through every widget so corners feel attached to the document, not pasted on. Keyboard: ⌘1–⌘4 open the four widgets; F fullscreens; Esc closes panels or exits fullscreen; ⌘S saves; / focuses any visible search.
