Status-only top bar

A 24-pixel readout. No buttons. Everything else is the palette.

The whole top bar is typography: six monospace status chips that report PHP, WordPress, storage, blueprint, save-state, and the playground's name — plus a faint ⌘K hint at the far right. Clicking any chip focuses the palette pre-scoped to that fact (clicking the PHP chip opens /settings → PHP; clicking the save-state chip opens /save). Status pills are not "buttons that look like text" — the cursor is a default arrow, and what they do is jump to the place where you'd change them. The only persistent button anywhere on screen is a single 32px fullscreen pill in the bottom-right corner of the iframe (one-click, ⌘ · to toggle, Esc to exit).

This playground runs WordPress 6.8 on PHP 8.3 in English (US). Network requests are enabled, multisite is off, and storage is browser (OPFS) with 192 MB in use. Pressing Apply & Reset wipes the database and reboots with the chosen versions.

⚠ Apply & Reset will erase this site

Switching WordPress will destroy the SQLite database and re-boot from a clean install. An automatic safety snapshot will be saved first — you can restore it from the toast for the next 24 hours.
