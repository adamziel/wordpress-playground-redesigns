v5 · #007 · Quarter-pie FAB

The chrome is one ball.

A single floating action ball sits in the bottom-right corner. Press-and-hold (or click) and six color-coded spokes radiate up and to the left in a quarter-pie. Drag your thumb across the arc, release on a spoke — that's the entire navigation model. Every panel (Refresh, Save, Sites, Files, Blueprint, Logs) collapses to one verb. One drawer surfaces below the spokes; its content swaps. iframe stays primary.

Direction #007 · extends #06 Mobile-first
Idle affordances: 4 · target ≤ 8
States shown: 3 · desktop & mobile

The six spokes

Refresh — reboot iframe1
Save — persist to OPFS2
Sites — launcher + saved + start-new3
Files — wp-content browser + editor4
Blueprint — JSON + Run5
Logs — debug.log stream6

Why a pie

Pie menus are directional: each spoke has a fixed angle, so you build muscle memory fast. Six spokes in a quarter-pie keep angular spread > 18° per slice — well above Fitts' threshold.

The seventh affordance, Settings, is a long-press on Sites (since it's the home of site identity). The eighth, fullscreen, is a one-key toggle (F) in the address bar.

What's gone

1No top toolbar (only a slim address pill).
2No left sidebar. No right launcher drawer separate from sites.
3No quick-settings popover + Site-Manager-Settings-tab + inline notice — Save is one verb (spoke 2), Settings is one place (long-press on Sites).
4One swappable drawer, six contents. Same width, same close behavior.

State 7. Interaction detail · the press-and-drag arc. How the gesture actually feels.
Gesture

Three ways to invoke the same six commands.

  1. Press-and-drag. Press the FAB. Spokes bloom in ~120ms. Drag your thumb across the arc; the spoke under it highlights and labels grow. Release → that spoke commits. Same gesture works with a mouse (mousedown + drag).
  2. Click. A simple click on the FAB opens the pie persistently. Click any spoke to commit. Click the FAB again (or press Esc) to close.
  3. Keyboard. Press 1–6 at any time. No need to open the pie — the keys map directly. Hold ⌥ to access the second layer (⌥3 = Settings).
Why a quarter-pie, not a full one? A full pie would arc over the iframe (the hero). A quarter-pie in the bottom-right hugs the corner: the iframe stays unobscured above the diagonal, and the arc respects right-handed thumb ergonomics on phones.
