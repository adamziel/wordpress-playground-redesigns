N Design notes — why breadcrumbs as IA

One primitive, one secondary surface. The trail is the only navigation primitive in the design — no tabs, no sidebar, no rail, no bottom dock. The single secondary surface is one pane that mounts under the leaf crumb. When the path is playground › Stylish Coffee, no pane is shown — the iframe is fullscreen-minus-44px. Any deeper path mounts the pane.

Every crumb is a hover-dropdown. Clicking a crumb navigates to that depth (popping the trail back). Hovering a crumb opens a dropdown of siblings — so you can switch Settings → Logs without first popping back to Stylish Coffee. This is the design's central interaction; the same gesture moves both shallower (click) and laterally (hover→click sibling).

Three settings doors collapse to one path. The popover, the sidebar tab, and the info notice all become a single leaf: › Settings. The destructive Apply & Reset action is gated behind a typed-name confirm (see panel D), not a small banner.

Lessons applied from prior v5 reviews.

  • Review 001 (#001): "show the expansion gesture as a first-class state." Panels B, C and the leaf dropdown in panel E render three different crumb-dropdowns explicitly — including the leaf-crumb-as-file-switcher in E — so the morph is teachable, not hidden.
  • Review 002 (#002): "don't stack three sections inside one pane." The root-crumb dropdown uses a 3-way segmented control (Saved / New / Import) instead of three scrollable sections; the rest is grouped under proper section headers.
  • Review 006 (#006): "don't import a clever idea from a different direction that undermines your restraint." No frosted Control Center, no Cmd+K palette, no spatial dock, no auto-hide HUD. The trail is the entire IA.
  • Review 011 (#011): "if you auto-hide, give a one-shot teaching state." Not applicable here — chrome never auto-hides at idle. Only fullscreen mode hides chrome, and it always shows the exit-pill (panel L).
  • Review 013 (#013): "the fullscreen toggle must be a visible icon, not keyboard-only." A maximize icon sits on every top strip; F is just a shortcut.
  • Review 015 (#015): "CLI restraint doesn't justify keyboard-only mobile." Mobile crumbs are full tap targets; sibling dropdowns become bottom sheets (not shown but consistent with the metaphor).
  • Review 018 (#018): "cite prior v5 reviews by number and lesson." Done — six lessons applied above with specific evidence.
