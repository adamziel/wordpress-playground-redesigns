v5 · #004 · iPad Stage Manager lite

One playground on stage. Peers recessed left. Saved sites docked below.

Apple's Stage Manager metaphor applied to WordPress Playground: the iframe is one focused "app" at center stage; two scaled-down peers wait on the left; a frosted dock at the bottom holds every saved site plus the Launcher tile and the lone "Site Manager" pull-tab on the right edge. Press F or click the chevron to fullscreen the iframe; Esc exits.

Same metaphor on a 390-pt screen.

The left "recessed stack" becomes a thin vertical sliver — peek-only thumbnails. The dock stays at the bottom; sites + the New tile sit side-by-side. The Site Manager pull-tab becomes a bottom sheet (with the iframe poking out 90 px so it's never invisible — the iframe-≥-65% rule of the parent direction stays honored here even if it's not the headline lesson for #04).

On both phones and iPads, the metaphor lands without extra explanation: app-stage, peers behind, dock below. Stage Manager works because every iPad user already understands it; this design lifts that literacy.

Three states shown: working session, launcher open, Site Manager open with Logs tab active.
