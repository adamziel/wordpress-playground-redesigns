# 008 — Apple Vision / Spatial

## Thesis
The WordPress Playground becomes a **spatial workspace**: the iframe is the principal "window" floating in 3D space, and every tool — settings, blueprint browser, file tree, logs, address bar — is a translucent glass slab that hovers at its own depth around it. Panels are summoned with a glance and dismissed with a gesture. There is no fixed chrome; the interface is volumetric and the user *looks at* the thing they want to use. The whole composition uses CSS `perspective`, `translateZ`, and `backdrop-filter` to give every surface a real sense of distance, refraction, and parallax — like visionOS, but for a WordPress sandbox.

## What I remixed / merged / removed
The top toolbar dissolves into the **WindowBar** — a small floating glass capsule pinned to the iframe panel itself (refresh, address bar, name, save, settings, site-manager, launcher). The Site Manager, settings popover, and launcher drawer are unified into a single language of **floating glass slabs** that the user pulls forward and pushes back. Tabs in the Site Manager become a vertical "Ornament" — a column of icon pills sliding along the panel's edge, matching visionOS's window ornaments. The "three settings entry points" pain point goes away: the Settings ornament always opens the same glass card, whether it's invoked from the WindowBar or the Site Manager. The blueprint gallery, instead of being buried inside the launcher, is its own **Constellation** slab that floats at z=-200 behind the iframe and animates forward on demand. Logs become a horizontal **Strip** docked under the iframe with severity color-coded chips.

## Trade-offs
Real spatial UI on a flat screen is a stylistic choice, not a literal one — heavy use of `backdrop-filter: blur()` and stacked `transform: translateZ()` is GPU-hungry and may shimmer on older machines (we degrade to flat panels under `prefers-reduced-motion`). The 3D parallax also adds a learning curve: there is no permanent left sidebar, so first-time users must discover the ornament dock. On mobile (390×844) the spatial metaphor flattens completely — slabs become a stacked sheet-deck with a bottom ornament rail, sacrificing the signature look but keeping every feature reachable in one tap. Finally, glass-on-photographic-backdrop is gorgeous but can hurt contrast; we pair every glass card with a strong inner stroke and a high-opacity content scrim under text.
