## Thesis
**The Playground Press.** WordPress Playground reframed as a daily broadsheet newspaper. The live WordPress iframe is today's lede — a full-bleed front-page article above the fold — and every other Playground surface becomes editorial furniture: the blueprint gallery is the features section, "Your Playgrounds" is the masthead's "Local Edition" column, settings live in the editor's stylebook, logs are the police blotter, files are the printing-press copy desk. Typography is the design: serif headlines (Playfair Display) for hierarchy, all-caps spurs (Bebop / IBM Plex), drop caps, hairline rules, column rules, classifieds, datelines. The whole UI is reorganized as one continuous broadsheet you read top-to-bottom rather than a chrome that surrounds the iframe.

## What I remixed, merged, removed
I **merged** the top toolbar into a real newspaper masthead — refresh becomes "Reload Edition", the address bar is the dateline subline, save is "Print This Issue", the playground name is the broadsheet title. The settings popover and Site Manager Settings tab **merged** into a single right-rail "Stylebook" column visible on the front page (no separate Site Manager mode — its tabs become sections of the paper that scroll into view: *Files* = Copy Desk, *Blueprint* = Editorial Brief, *Database* = Records Bureau, *Logs* = Police Blotter). The "Saved Playgrounds" launcher **merged** into the Classifieds / Local Edition / Features sections of page two, accessed by scrolling or via the Front Page nav strip — no drawer. Additional actions (Export to GitHub PR, Download .zip) live in a "Syndication" box. Mobile collapses the broadsheet into a tabloid-style stacked feed with a bottom paper-edge nav. I **removed** the three duplicate settings entry points by consolidating them into one Stylebook rail, and I **removed** the modal "Apply & Reset" red-banner by making it a galley-proof confirmation card in the stylebook itself.

## Trade-offs
The biggest risk: serif broadsheet typography reads as *slow*, deliberately archival — perfect for a sandbox you sit with, hostile to "just give me a button now". I leaned in because Playground's job is exploration, not productivity. The newspaper IA collapses what was previously hidden behind tabs into a long-scroll page; that's a big information-density bet and could overwhelm first-time users. The mitigation is the front-page nav strip (acts like newspaper section flags) which jumps you between Lede, Features, Local Edition, Copy Desk, Records, Blotter, Stylebook. Cards lose hover-states' worth of micro-affordances because the visual language is print-static; I add subtle ink-bleed hover treatments to keep it alive. And: print metaphors invite cuteness — "Police Blotter" for logs is a joke that might wear thin, so the underlying labels stay explicit ("Logs — error stream") on hover and in mobile.
