# 007 — Tokaido Lacquer

**Thesis.** A Japanese product manual rendered in dark mode: lacquered black, one rusted vermilion seal, and five hand-cut glyphs that genuinely look different from each other. The locked Quiet-Rail interaction model gets a calligraphic skin — Newsreader italic for the things that name themselves (pane titles, site names, the identity mark, blueprint counts), and Plus Jakarta Sans for everything that has a job to do (forms, log lines, paths, helpers). The rail is 68 px wide, the pane is 440–540 px, and the iframe sits sharp in the middle with no chrome competing for its attention.

**Why is the iframe the loudest object on the screen?** Because vermilion appears in exactly two places on the entire surface — the 4 px square beside the active dock and the Saved-state pill — so every other thing is ink-on-lacquer. The iframe is the only fully-saturated, fully-sharp region of the screen, and the only colour the eye can lock onto outside it is a fingernail-sized seal whose only job is to tell you which dock is live.

**What's locked vs explored.** Locked: the 5-dock rail with one identity mark, the single swap pane (never two side-by-side), the slim top capsule with refresh / address / WP Admin / Homepage / playground name / save pill / More / Fullscreen (8 affordances or fewer), the corner site widget, the modal Library, the mobile mirror at 390 × 844. Explored: the rail glyphs as hand-built SVG woodblock marks (horizon line over water · key · folded paper crease · stacked-stone cairn · single brush stroke) so the five docks are individually recognisable without labels; vermilion as a single-frequency accent used twice; serif italic typography for naming and proportional sans for working.

**Trade-offs.** The hand-built glyphs require the user to learn a small vocabulary — they're more recognisable than a generic icon set once learned, less recognisable in the first three seconds. We counter that with the title attribute (hover tooltip) and with the pane title that always echoes the active dock's noun. Newsreader italic at small sizes is less crisp than a geometric sans, but the lacquered idiom needs the calligraphic tilt to feel like the seal-script echo it's borrowing from. Vermilion only-on-Saved-and-seal means the rest of the save-pill cycle (Unsaved · Saving · Stale) reads in neutral / saving-amber / dashed-rule — the user must learn the cycle rather than relying on traffic-light colour.
