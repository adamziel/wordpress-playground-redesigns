# 008 — Monastery Scriptorium

**Thesis.** A hand-illuminated manuscript at candlelight: dark vellum, single gilt initial per chapter, ink-secondary quill flourishes for the awake one. The locked Quiet-Rail interaction model is dressed as the index page of a chained manuscript — five illuminated capitals S, M, B, D, L stand watch on the rail in Cormorant Garamond 28 px, and the active one fills gilt while a sparse hand-drawn pen curl wraps around it. Atkinson Hyperlegible — a face engineered specifically for legibility on dark backgrounds — does every other job on the screen: forms, log lines, paths, helpers, table contents.

**Why is the iframe the loudest object on the screen?** Because gilt (#B3893D) appears in exactly two places — the active capital's fill and the Saved-state pill — and every other surface is ink-on-vellum. The rail is read as a page index rather than a control deck, so cognitive load drops to "pick a chapter": cognitively, the iframe is the chapter you are already inside, and the rail is a list of the others. Your eye lands on the only chromatic, fully-saturated region of the screen.

**What's locked vs explored.** Locked: 5-dock rail with one identity mark, single swap pane, slim top capsule with refresh / address / WP Admin / Homepage / playground name / save pill / More / Fullscreen (8 affordances or fewer), corner site widget, modal Library, mobile mirror at 390 × 844. Explored: the rail as a column of illuminated capitals rather than icons or monograms (so each dock is unambiguously distinct in silhouette and reads as a chapter heading); a hand-drawn quill flourish in inline SVG curling around the active letter to mark "awake" without a heavy outline; type discipline — Cormorant Garamond is forbidden outside rail + pane titles, Atkinson Hyperlegible everywhere else, ensuring legibility doesn't collapse at body sizes against dark backgrounds.

**Trade-offs.** Cormorant Garamond at 28 px is gorgeous but the letters S, M, B, D, L share more visual mass than icons would — the silhouettes are distinct enough at a glance but a brand-new user will probably read the first letter of each label rather than memorise the form. Hover tooltips counter that. The gilt-on-dark colour can be hard to read at small sizes; we never use it for body, only for the 28 px capital and the 6 px square in the save pill. The quill flourish is intentionally sparse — flashy curls would compete with the iframe, defeating the entire idiom — so the &ldquo;active&rdquo; cue is genuinely subtle and users may rely on the gilt fill more than the flourish.
